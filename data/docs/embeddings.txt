Understanding Embeddings

An embedding is a list of numbers that represents the meaning of a piece of text. 

In this project each embedding has 384 numbers, produced by the all-MiniLM-L6-v2 model from the sentence-transformers library.

The key idea is that text with similar meaning produces similar numbers. 

The sentence "the cat sat on the mat" and "a feline rested on the rug" will have embeddings that point in almost the same direction, 

even though they share no words. 

This is what lets a search engine find results by meaning instead of by exact keyword.

To compare two embeddings we use cosine similarity. 

It measures the angle between the two vectors and returns a score from -1 to 1. 

A score of 1.0 means identical meaning, a score near 0 means unrelated, and a negative score means opposite meaning. 

Retrieval works by embedding the question and returning the stored chunks with the highest cosine similarity.

Cosine Similarity Explained

Cosine similarity is the math that powers search by meaning. 

It takes two embedding vectors and measures the angle between them, 

ignoring how long the vectors are and caring only about the direction they point.

The formula is the dot product of the two vectors divided by the product of their 

lengths. In Python with NumPy this is a single line, 

which is one reason NumPy is used in this project. 

The result is always between -1 and 1.

A result of 1.0 means the two texts point in exactly the same direction and share the 

same meaning. A result near 0 means the texts are unrelated. 

Because meaning is captured by direction rather than magnitude, 

cosine similarity works well even when one piece of text is much longer than the other. 

The retriever ranks every stored chunk by its cosine similarity to the question and 

returns the top few.
